TPACK (Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge)

Module : I.C.T

Author : Abdelmalek ELBOUDARY

Year : 2024

üß† TPACK, or Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge, is a framework that emphasizes the integration of technology, pedagogy (teaching methods), and content knowledge to enhance teaching and learning. Developed by Punya Mishra and Matthew Koehler, TPACK recognizes the interconnectedness of these three domains and emphasizes the importance of effectively integrating technology into educational practices to promote meaningful learning experiences.

üîç TPACK expands upon the traditional notions of pedagogical content knowledge (PCK) and technological content knowledge (TCK) by integrating them into a comprehensive framework. It highlights the need for educators to possess not only subject matter expertise (content knowledge) and knowledge of effective teaching strategies (pedagogical knowledge) but also an understanding of how to effectively leverage technology to support and enhance teaching and learning (technological knowledge).

üéì TPACK provides educators with a holistic framework for designing and implementing technology-rich learning environments that are aligned with instructional goals and promote student engagement, collaboration, and critical thinking. By integrating technology seamlessly into their teaching practices, educators can create dynamic and interactive learning experiences that cater to the diverse needs and learning styles of their students, ultimately fostering deeper learning and knowledge construction.
